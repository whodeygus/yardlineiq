Seahawks @ Cardinals
High
Pick: Seahawks -1.0
Game Time: Thursday, 8:15 PM EST
Analysis: This is a BIG NFC West Divison matchup on primetime TV. Both the Cardinals and the Seahawks come in to Thursday night looking to keep up with the often-leading 49ers. Interestingly enough, the West has been a dogfight thus far as even the Rams are 2-1 so games like this are of utmost importance early in the season. We believe the difference in this matchup will be Seattle's front 7. While the secondary can be vulnerable at times, the front 7 should look to dominate the Cardinals offensive front. This will force Kyler Murray to beat them and we're just not convinced that he can. Take the Seahwaks tonight -1. Play up to -2.5
Minnesota @ Pittsburgh
Medium
Pick: Minnesota -2.5
Game Time: Sunday 9:30 AM EST
Analysis: The NFL travels to Dublin, Ireland in a matchup between the Vikings and the Steelers. While the Rooney family are the former ambassadors to Ireland, look for their to in fact be a large contingent of Steelers fans in the building come Sunday morning. While we can justify this game going either way, the model definitely favors the Vikings and there are several reasons why. JJ McCarthy will be out once again for the Vikings which means Carson Wentz will get another shot to prove he's still a starter in the league. In order to cover the spread, the Vikings must get the ground game going early. The Steelers have an Elite pass rush so running the football will help combat that. In clear passing downs, Wentz must get the ball out of his hands quickly. Additionally, they must continue to create turnovers at a high rate. Defensive coordinator Brian Flores has shown how well he can gameplan an opponent and his familiarity with the Steelers gives even more confidence to backing the Vikings. While anything can happen when teams have to travel so far, look for this game to trend toward the Vikings. Take Minnesota -2.5.
Jaguars @ 49ers
Medium
Pick: 49ers -3.5
Game Time: Sunday 4:05 PM EST
Analysis: The Jaguars travel to San Francisco this week in a game that will see the 49ers get their starting QB Brock Purdy back. It won't be easy for Purdy this week as Jacksonville's defensive line will run many different looks to confuse the 49ers upfront. What we believe San Francisco will do is establish the run game with Christian McCaffrey and ultimately that well open up play action as well as the intermediate passing game to George Kittle and others. The 49ers must control the trenches and although their defensive front is strong, the Model is very confident that San Francisco can do enough to get the job done at home. Take the 49ers -3.5.
Colts @ Rams
Medium
Pick: Rams -3.5
Game Time: Sunday 4:05 PM EST
Analysis: The Colts travel west to LA to take on the Rams in Week 4. The Colts, fresh off of yet another victory en route to a perfect 3-0 start look to continue their hot streak against Sean McVay and the Rams. Unfortunately for them, the Rams are 2-1 and coming off of a heartbreaking defeat at the hands of the defending SuperBowl Champion Eagles where they quite literally snatched a defeat from the jaws of victory. This week, they look to right the ship and in order to do so, they must contain the Colts run game. Indy will most certainly bring a heavy dose of Jonathan Taylor and work in the play action whenever needed. I do believe what Coach McVay will do is lean in on the efficiency of Matthew Stafford and work in the intermediate to longer passing game and will exploit mismatches in the middle of the field where Indy often struggles. Look for Stafford to help control the tempo and make quick, decisive reads. This, in our opinion is Must-Watch TV as this game will show a lot about both teams. As for now, the model says take the Rams -3.5.
Bears @ Raiders
Medium
Pick: Bears +1.5
Game Time: 4:25 PM EST
Analysis: The Bears come in fresh off of a victory against the Cowboys and looking to continue their newfound momentum. The Raiders on the other hand continue to struggle. After opening the season 1-0, they have dropped back to back games by multiple scores and have had trouble hanging on to the football when on offense. While the Bears aren't quite ready to make a run at the division crown, they certainly aren't throwing in the towel as they have a young and hungry roster that has something to prove. The Raiders on the other hand don't feel as though they are hungry enough. As for the model, it favors the Bears on Sunday Afternoon. Take the Bears +1.5 or Moneyline.
